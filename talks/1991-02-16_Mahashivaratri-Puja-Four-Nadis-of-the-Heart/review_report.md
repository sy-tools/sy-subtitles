### Critic Filter
| Source | # | Verdict | Reason |
|--------|---|---------|--------|
| L | L1 | **Remove** | «Воно» legitimately refers back to the neuter «те» established in «Любов – це **те**, що виправляє…»; reading it as an abstract impersonal subject is defensible. Not a clear grammatical error — changing it is an interpretive rewrite, contrary to the minimal-change principle. |
| L | L2 | **Remove** | The colon construction mirrors the source ("to see why: why I want this?"). Here «чому» is used nominally ("the why") immediately before the colon and its elaboration, so no comma is required. |
| S | S1 | **Keep** | `glossary/CLAUDE.md` explicitly lists **Істина** (absolute Truth) as uppercase, and the review brief names it among spiritual terms to check. Here «Він є істина» states Truth as God's essence = absolute Truth. Consistent with the project's demonstrated practice of capitalizing divine-essence terms even where English is lowercase (cf. Дух for "spirit"). любов stays lowercase (not a listed term, lowercase everywhere in the text). |
| S | S2 | **Remove** | EN source is lowercase ("nirvana") throughout; used as a generic spiritual goal, repeated as "your nirvana". Internally consistent (always lowercase) and parallel with other lowercase concepts (самадхі, мокша, бхакті). The `terms_lookup.yaml` citation form `Нірвана` is not binding for running-text common-noun use. |
| S | S3 | **Remove** | Same reasoning as S2. `тур’я` is one of a parallel set of state names — джаґруті, сушупті, тур’я — all lowercase in §§87–88, matching the lowercase EN. Capitalizing only `тур’я` would break that parallel. |
| S | S4 | **Remove** | The *named* Pujas are already correctly capitalized (Пуджа Махашіваратрі §§2,6; Пуджа Шіви §97; Пуджу Ґанеші §100). The generic «в інших пуджах / в цій пуджі» (§97) is a common-noun use matching EN lowercase "pujas"; there is no mid-sentence uppercase generic counterpart, so the text is internally consistent. (§100 "Пуджа…" is sentence-initial, so its capital is automatic and not evidence either way.) |
