27 September 1979
Navaratri Puja
Hinduja Auditorium, Mumbai (India)
Talk Language: Hindi | Translation (Hindi to English)

The subject that has been placed before you today is most fascinating and absorbing: Kundalini and Jesus Christ, Lord Jesus Christ. For ordinary, common people this is something entirely new, and till this day no one has ever drawn any connection between the two in this manner.
The reason for this is that whenever flowers have blossomed upon the tree of Dharma, they have spread their fragrance through many provinces, many countries, and many languages. But what relationship they bore to one another — that only the tree itself can know. Wherever they blossomed, wherever their fragrance spread, the people there never truly accepted them. So how, then, could others ever know or understand that these flowers all bloom upon one and the same tree?
Now I am speaking to you from that direction to which you have not yet been brought. Once you reach there, you will understand My words very easily. For at present you have no way of knowing whether what I am saying is true or untrue; you possess no proof of it. How will you know whether what I say is true or false? The way to know it is this — and the difficulty is that you have not yet learned to discover your own meaning. You are not yet at that level where you have become connected with the Truth.
For this very reason you cannot tell whether a thing is true or false, whether it is mere legend or something fashioned by the mind. Those who, as we say, have become realized — they can come to know fully which thing is true and which is false. Because of the vibrations that flow from their hands — the experiment we conducted here yesterday, when these vibrations began to flow from the hands of so many people — if you observe and weigh this carefully, you will understand that what I am telling you is the complete Truth.
Christians know of Jesus Christ only through the Bible, which is a most profound book. Its depth is so great that, being unable to grasp its meaning, these people make all sorts of attempts at interpretation. For instance, it is written in the Bible, "I will appear before you like tongues of flames." They cannot give the meaning of this. They imagine that when God appears before them, such flames will come pouring out of Him. But that is not its meaning. Its meaning is this: I shall become visible to you in the Sahasrara. It is a description of the Sahasrara. The Kundalini is described in many ways in the Bible.
I can give you countless quotations from it. Jesus Christ has said, "Those who are not against Me are with Me" — those who are not in opposition to Me are with Me. If you go and ask the Christians who these people are, they do not know. They have made it out that Jesus Christ said, "I am the path, I am the door" — I am the way and I am the door. But He did not say, "I am the destination" — He never said that He was the goal. If He said, "I am the way and I am the door," then the destination must be something beyond.
Even after speaking in such profound language, the wicked people of that time — within four years, they let Him live only four years — within four years they nailed Him upon the cross. And even today, those who believe in Him and say that they follow His religion go on, every single day, sacrificing all that He truly stood for.
As I told you yesterday, the Hindus are just as foolish, and so are the Muslims, and so are the Christians. About this Jesus Christ — if you could ever read the Devi Bhagavata, you would discover there that Shri Radha created Him out of Her own inner strength, and gave Him the name Mahavishnu. She made it in two parts — She formed it as an egg-shape. The base of this egg-shape is Shri Ganesha. It is Shri Ganesha who is fundamentally the power of Jesus Christ. And the other part of the egg-shape — the one we call Shadanana, Shanmukhanana — that is called Kartikeya.
The very life-breath within this whole egg is Jesus Christ. The complete Brahma tattwa, the Omkara — that itself is Jesus Christ. The entire existence of the Kundalini is, one may say, because of Him. And it is He who is seated at the very base of the Kundalini.
Shri Radha gave Him birth. You will come to know this if you read the Devi Purana. But where are our modern people of today ready to read Markandeya! You will not find a seer like Markandeya however much you may search. He has described the most subtle, intricate things about the Devi — what the marks of the Devi are, how the Devi dresses, what the Devi delights in, how She is, Her ways and bearing, Her nature. Sometimes one is filled with wonder at how he came to know so much.
But our modern people of today, educated in English learning, are not even willing to accept that there exists in this world such a thing as the Devi, or such a thing as divine power. Although today the English are turning in this direction, our Indians are still being very great Englishmen. I say many times that, leaving aside the country of the English themselves, there are more Englishmen here in our India.
I think very few among you would have read the Markandeya Purana. But if you read in it the establishment of Mahavishnu — if you give your attention to that — you will understand that this whole thing is none other than a description of Christ, of the Christ.
Shri Radha created Him by Her own power, which is the power of Mahalakshmi. And His mother, who was Mary, was none other than — you should simply understand this directly — Shri Radha Herself. It was Shri Radha Herself, in person, who gave birth to Him. Radha — "Ra" means energy, Shakti; and "dha" means the one who holds and sustains it. She who held the power within Herself, She is Radha. This Radha is a form of Mahalakshmi.
Shri Radha incarnated upon this earth. She who was earlier Shri Sita became Shri Radha. And it was Shri Radha who took Shri Ganesha into Her lap — because Shri Radha is the Incarnation of Mahalakshmi, and Shri Ganesha had been created by Mahakali, in the form of Gauri — She took Him into Her lap, and giving Him this special form, She made Him Krishta. Now this word "Krishta" too comes from Krishna, because His father is Shri Krishna; that is why He was called Krishta. This is something to be understood very deeply.
And His name Jesus was created in this way too: the thought came to Shri Radha that the name Yashoda had never appeared anywhere until now. Yashoda — "yash" meaning fame and "de" meaning to give, the one who gives fame, and so Yashoda. But if we look here in our own country, He is called Yeshu and Jeshu, in short form. If the name Yashoda is used in U.P. and such places, it is shortened to Jasu. That very name, Jasu, is what She gave to Him. In this way the name Jesus Christ came into being.
Now Hindu people feel that Jesus Christ has, after all, become one of our own. But are you even Hindu? You are not even really Indian. And Hindutva does not mean to quarrel over some thing — that is not Hindutva at all. Hindutva is understood to belong to the worshippers of Shakti. Only those who are flooded with Shakti, who are cultured and sanctified by Shakti, are to be regarded as Hindus. Those within whom the Shakti has not yet been awakened cannot be regarded as Hindus.
In this way you can understand that Jesus Christ is a very great Incarnation, created by Shri Radha, from the time of Vaikuntha. It is described of Him that Shri Radha created Him. Out of that, He was then divided into two parts. One part She left just as it was, which was the part of Shri Ganesha. And the other part She gave a special form — that egg She gave the form of a child. Then this child kept weeping for many years. He remembered his father. When his father saw him — that is, when Krishna, Shri Krishna, saw him — He granted him many boons.
One of these boons is this: "You shall sit higher than Me." See, our Vishuddhi chakra is here, and our Agnya chakra is here. But in the brain, the Vishuddhi chakra is here. The seat of the Vishuddhi chakra inside the brain is here. Yet in terms of the chakras, Shri Krishna's chakra is this one, and above it, here, is the Agnya chakra, where Jesus Christ is seated.
The second thing He said was: "You shall remain the foundation of the entire world." The third thing He said was: "Whatever I receive in My Puja, a sixteenth part of it already goes to you first." In this way, after granting many boons, the Virata, Shri Krishna, gave Him permission to take incarnation in this world.
When Jesus Christ speaks of his father, He is speaking of Krishna, He is speaking of the Virata. He always spoke of his father, because his father had not incarnated together with him in this world. His mother was, in person, the Adi Shakti of Mahalakshmi Herself. It is She whom He called the Holy Ghost. But at that time He did not speak of the Holy Ghost, and for this reason: He did not reveal His mother to the world as the Holy Ghost or as any Goddess. Because if the wicked rakshasas of that time had come to know that she is the Holy Ghost, they would have gone after her. And had such a situation arisen, then Jesus Christ Himself would have become so enraged that He would have taken up all the powers of destruction that He has and laid everything utterly to waste.
Jesus Christ has eleven Rudras, meaning eleven powers of destruction. They are the eleven destructive powers upon our forehead. These eleven destructive powers — when the Incarnation of Kalki comes, about which I am going to tell you tomorrow — will then completely carry out whatever work of destruction is to be done in this world. These eleven destructive powers of His are seated in our forehead. Among them, one power is that of Shri Hanumana, and one power is that of Shri Bhairavanatha, who are called Gabriel and Michael in the Bible.
Even if you say it in English, you can still awaken that power through the mantra. Or whether you say it in Hindi or in Sanskrit, that power can still be awakened within you. That is to say, on your right side, the Pingala nadi, the Hanumana power flows. When your right side is not working and there is trouble in it, then one has to take the name of Hanumana. At that time, even if you take the name of Gabriel, that power still begins to work.
As for the power on the left side, there the power of Saint Michael — or the one whom we call Shri Bhairavanatha — flows. If you wish to set the left side working, then you have to take the support of Shri Bhairavanatha. Even if you take the name of Saint Michael, you can still set this power working. Or you can test it through the mantra; you can verify for yourself whether what I am saying is correct or not.
In this way you can understand that those who consider themselves Christians, or Hindus, or Muslims, and quarrel among themselves over worthless matters, are utterly foolish. Because, having grown upon one and the same tree, all these are relatives of one another. Jesus Christ has said that "those who are not against me are with me." All of this is speaking of those deities who reside upon our various chakras. They are all relatives of one another. It is only our own relationship that does not connect with them. So we cling to each one separately — just as you would pluck a flower off a tree, draw out its living essence, and then go on fighting among ourselves holding those dead flowers. This is the kind of state we are in.
You will be surprised to know that in Sahaja Yoga the awakening of the Kundalini depends very much upon the Agnya chakra. Most of the people who are moving about in the world these days carry a heavy stamp of ego upon them, because, by and large, we get caught up in our activities through the ego. It is the ego that pushes us into something, and it is to feed that very ego that we have gone on doing whatever we have done. So if this ego is to be set right, we have to remember Lord Jesus Christ.
When I apply sindoor to each one of you here, you will be surprised to know that it is the very chakra of Lord Jesus Christ that I am moving, the very chakra that I am awakening. This tika that you see on My forehead is the tika of His very blood; it is the symbol of it. That is why this red colour carries such great significance.
In this blood lies that symbol which showed that God gave His only begotten Son to this world, and you drenched Him in blood. It is His sign, so that you may repent, and feel sorrow over your foolishness and over your wickedness, and reflect upon it: what is this that we have done?
We think that Shri Mataji has applied sindoor, or that we have applied sindoor, so it must be some sign of the Hindus. But if you read about John's Revelation in the Bible, you will understand, for it is written there that this mark will remain upon those people when Kalki comes, mounted upon a white horse, carrying these eleven Rudras. They did not write down the name of Kalki; they said that He will have His very own name, and that He alone will know it. But when He comes upon the white horse, then they will come to know whose mark is upon your forehead. By that mark they will recognize who this is.
There is even a great deal said about Me in it — in what manner Sahaja Yoga will come about, and in what manner people will receive it, and in what manner the Holy Ghost Himself will come and grant these people their realization. Because the Incarnation of Jesus Christ took place before us, He has spoken about this matter in the most elaborate detail. Just as Mohammed Sahib explained, in a very elaborate manner, the awakening of the Kundalini and the warding off of evil forces and so on, describing their workings; in the very same way Jesus Christ explained, in a very simple manner, what the weapon of a human being is. According to Him, the greatest weapon of a human being is to forgive.
That which works invisibly in the principle of Shri Ganesha, the same thing comes into the human principle and works in the form of forgiveness. Forgiveness is in truth a very great weapon, by which a human being is saved from his own ego. Suppose someone has given you great sorrow, or insulted you, or troubled you, or caused you harm — then your ego is wounded by it. At that moment, if you hold any such feeling in your mind towards him, or if you keep thinking in this way, that this man did such an injustice to me, and you go on dwelling on him day and night, then by such useless brooding you will suffer a great deal, and the whole time that man will remain active within you. Even though he may have forgotten the whole thing, and it may have left no effect upon him at all, you, by remembering that matter, will go on making yourself miserable again and again, and you will go on playing into his hands.
And when we say, forgive everyone — to forgive everyone is the supreme instrument of a human being — then what is it that we actually do? This too is a very great falsehood, that we forgive — so what is it that we do? It is a myth. What do we do? You have told someone, "I have forgiven you" — what have you done? You have done nothing. You have only saved your own self, from the trouble of the other and from brooding upon the other.
As long as you keep dwelling painfully upon that man, you yourself will remain miserable. Therefore, forgiveness is for us a very simple and straightforward instrument, by which we save ourselves from the torments of others.
This very great instrument lies within Jesus Christ. Jesus Christ possesses those powers — as I have told you — within whom the eleven Rudras reside. And yet He hung Himself upon the cross, He let Himself be hung upon it. Why did He hang Himself upon the cross, when He had all the powers? He could have slain everyone and could have shattered everyone to pieces.
And right there, where the Adi Shakti, His own Mother, stood weeping beside Him — she could not bear it, because, after all, she was in a human birth. She could not bear it, that even though all the powers reside within me, these people are crucifying my child upon the cross, and that today I must stand here to witness this drama.
The truth of the matter is that such people neither endure any sorrow, nor become entangled in any pleasure; for them it is all a play and a drama. Coming into this world and mounting the cross, He showed what great fools these people are who crucified Him upon the cross. That is why He used to ride upon a donkey, and the meaning of this is that His mount was always the donkey.
The direct corollary of this is that when you have a pain in your head, when there is very great trouble, very great suffering, then you should say, "I have forgiven everyone." Do not take any medicine, do not apply any treatment to it. Only say this, that all the people in the world, however many there are, who have tormented me or who go on tormenting me to this day — I have forgiven them all. You will see, your headache will go away.
Especially for the Sahaja Yogis, who are kindled by this Kundalini Shakti, the work happens so quickly — because within you, Jesus Christ, who is the very embodiment of forgiveness, has been awakened.
Now see where His place is — it is upon the Agnya chakra. The Agnya chakra within us is at the optic chiasma, the place where the two nerves of our eyes cross each other; and there, in that very centre, is a point. It is so subtle even now that the doctors have not been able to see it.
But there is an extremely subtle door at that place. That is why He said, "I am the door." This door is so exceedingly subtle that, in order to pass through it from within, a personality like Jesus Christ has to come into this world and pass through it.
Because of our ego, we killed Him — because we could not bear that a human being should come as God. When some human being says, "I am God," man cannot tolerate it, and that is why he killed Him.
What wrong had He done? He did good to people the world over, He taught the whole world good things, He taught everyone to live with discipline, to live well, to put your life in proper order. He kept to the language of love, and we gave Him malice and suffering and troubles.
But those who teach you filthy things and lead you into sinful deeds, and tell you to go on doing wicked things — that the more you do them, the better you will become — such people who make fools of you, we read their books day and night and keep going to their feet.
You have seen it today — in these times the gurus we follow are each more cunning and brazen than the other — are they not? For them we squander our money, for them we are ready to do anything in the world. And if some such true man comes and stands before us, we crucify him.
To give such utterly foolish, egotistical people a very great lesson, to give them a mighty teaching, the Almighty God Himself sent His only begotten son into this world, and you nailed Him to the cross. Just look at these specimens of your intelligence — what marvels you have achieved! And in this very way you have done it every single time. Whenever any Incarnation came before, man went on doing exactly the same thing, and always ran away from the Truth — he kept clinging to what merely seemed good and kept fleeing from the True.
Today I hear that people go to Shirdi a great deal. Although Sainath has now departed from there, and they have made the place so filthy — even so, people keep going to Shirdi. They go to Shirdi precisely because Sainath is not present here today. When the Sainath of Shirdi was alive, He had nothing to eat. Today everyone will go to Shirdi. Why? Because now that He is dead, all is well. Because now the Sainath of Shirdi has become tied around our necks! "Our" Sainath of Shirdi — since when did He become yours?
This is the utter foolishness of man — to run after the one who has died. It was for this very foolishness and this very ego that Jesus Christ sacrificed Himself. And even today man goes on running in that same foolishness — leaving aside the one who is here in person, who is before him, who is the living proof. And he runs after such a one who has died, who is finished, and whose place today is somewhere else.
You have seen that many of the devotees of Shirdi who came here were asked to put the question — "Shri Mataji, are You Shirdinath?" — and the vibrations came within them. It is quite true. Whenever any Incarnation came, if you had asked the question, you would have received the answer. But you went on sitting with the one of the past who had died, and you went along leaning on him. Because he is not alive today, it suits us well to have him in our hands.
For instance, I told you the story of Gyaneshwara — that here a very grand temple of Gyaneshwara has been built. I said, "Let us do contemplation here." They said, "No, sir, here you must do the dindi, here you must dance." I said, "Did he do that? This idol that you have set up here before you — what did he do?" Then they began to say, "His case was different; he was someone special." And I said, "And you — were you born only to do the dindi? He told you to contemplate even throughout this whole life, he told you to attain God. The cause, the root cause of ignorance, is that there is no light within you — it is for this that he spoke; do that. Why should you do this?" "No, no, Mataji, what You say is right, but this cannot be done here." I said, "If he were here today, I would speak with him — what is there for me to speak with you about? What a place I have come into, ending up with you." They did not let us contemplate, nor let us teach meditation, nor do anything — "only do this."
So why does a human being cling to what is gone, to the past? Because of his ego. Because through it his ego is doubled — "Brother, I am the disciple of so-and-so, of such-and-such." But if you will not accept the living proof that stands right here before you, then how is your hunger ever going to be satisfied? What fulfilment is ever going to come to you? Just think about this — sometimes one should think of one's own interest, one should think of one's own welfare. The Ganga is flowing here today, and you have gone off to sit somewhere where there is no Ganga, to fetch water from where the Ganga is not — and you say, "Brother, I am sitting in the Ganga." This is the kind of talk you make. The living proof that stands before you today — accept it.
The very same situation arose in the time of Jesus Christ. He made many attempts at Kundalini awakening, but they remained so unsuccessful that He cured only twenty-one people in all — whereas now, in Sahaja Yoga, thousands of people are being cured. He could have cured thousands, but the disciples He had were also great donkeys, also good for nothing. Those people only thought that Jesus Christ is a man who cures everything and does this healing — so, "Come, bring this one to Him too, bring that one to Him as well."
He said, "Brother, no more than this." When they saw Him walking upon the water — because He is the Pranava, the Omkara, that is why He could walk upon the water, and the Kundalini too is the Omkara itself — even when they saw Him walking on the water, these people still did not believe in Him.
With great difficulty He had gathered together barely twelve fishermen. And what else could He do? You hardly find anyone simpler than fishermen. Just look at our own group here. In those days, twelve fishermen were found only with the greatest difficulty. Nowadays, if you were to try to explain the Kundalini to fishermen, how on earth would you explain it! So you too can understand what those times were like — who was there to speak with Him? Nowadays people have become extremely intelligent; in those days they were of a very low order.
Somehow He explained it to them, He told them, yet they were not the kind to understand. All they wanted was, "You set this one right, set that one right." Just as it happens in our Sahaja Yoga too — the moment some person is given realization, at once he will say, "Mother, look, I have a sister, her husband has got such-and-such, will You cure him?" Then he comes back having taken a list of every sick person in the whole village. Why? Because now they have become our agents. Now they have taken up the agency. These too are all problems of the ego — all of them are problems of the ego.
A human being should understand that the ego is something very subtle. Now, the other way is that you fight your own ego. That way too is not right. By fighting the ego, you can never get anywhere. The ego has to be absorbed within of its own accord. When your attention, seated upon the Kundalini, piercing the Brahmarandhra, merges into that Virata, then the ego becomes a very part of that Virata. The ego that is in you people is false.
What do you do? Ask yourselves — what do you do in this world? Why, you take some dead thing and give it another form — what great work is this that you do? Can you make a fruit out of a flower? First ask the question of yourself. This nose, this mouth, this whole beautiful body and all else that you have been given — how did you come by it? You became a human being from an amoeba — how did that happen? With what beauty, without your even being aware of it, you were given all this.
It is the infinite, boundless grace of God that He created you and gave you this human form, this human embodiment. Towards Him, what can a human being give but gratitude? Towards Him, what indeed can a human being give but reverence?
What have you been doing till this day? What living work have you ever done? Take a test-tube baby — taking a living thing from somewhere, somehow nurturing and rearing that living thing and bringing it up — and over that the human being has become so full of ego! And that too — he who did not bring the living quality into being in the first place — he merely took the living quality from somewhere, just as we do a crossing, and crossed it in that way. How much ego the human being has acquired! He reached the moon, and how much ego he acquired! Before the One who made thousands of universes like this moon and this sun and set them in their places, what is your ego anyway? Your ego is false, but His ego is true. That great Ego — "Aham karoti," meaning, He exists, and it is He alone who does.
This is something I sometimes say in jest — that some people travelling in an aeroplane took their luggage up onto their own heads. People asked them, "Why have you put the luggage on your heads?" And they said, "We are lightening the load of this aeroplane a little." Your ego is just like that. The ego you have is false. You do nothing at all. To this day you have done nothing at all. Whatever you have done is all distorted. Let Him do it who is the One to do it and who really does it. You are only the instrument within His hands. Just as this instrument stands here before Me today — it does nothing, this you know. It is I who am speaking, I am speaking, and this one only stands in between as a witness. He is being drawn along by this energy.
In the very same way, you too are an instrument of God. He has made you. Let His power flow through you, and put your meaning, your purpose, to use. And therefore, to put this meaning to use, upon the Agnya chakra — which is the most difficult chakra, the subtlest principle, that of the Omkara, of the Pranava — that very One descended in person into this world, and that very One in person is Shri Jesus Christ.
Now the Kundalini has a relationship with this just as the moon has with its moonlight, or the sun with its sun-rays. The Kundalini, by her own power — Gauri, by her own power, created Shri Ganesha. By her own strength of mind, by her own penance, by her own merit, she made Him into Ganesha. When Ganesha had come to the stage where He could take incarnation, then Jesus Christ was born.
Now the people in England raise a great deal of argument about how He could have been born in this way — what they call divine conception — how can this be possible? I say, how little do these human beings understand, how little of all this do they really grasp! Do they ever even stop to think how a sprout breaks forth from a seed? How do you breathe? How does your movement, your functioning take place? Where does the energy in your brain come from? In what manner have you come into this world? Have you ever diagnosed any of this? You say that within the earth, in the depths of the ground, there is the force of gravity. But just consider for a moment — where did it come from? Just as little children might suddenly imagine themselves to be Shivaji, in the same way these notions of human beings seem to me rather childish. This power that seems to you to be active and working within yourselves — all this is maya, all this is illusion. You have not yet known the Truth; you will have to break through this illusion. Until this illusion of yours is broken, you will go on accepting this very illusion as the Truth. And this is the great illusion within you — that it is you yourselves who are carrying out all the work.
And to break this illusion, it was necessary to bring a human form into the world. For whenever, in this world, a human being or any other living creature has tried to take a step towards evolution, at that very time the Incarnations have come into the world. You yourselves know that Vishnu took His Incarnation, and coming into the world as Shri Rama He wandered from forest to forest, and to make you understand, to bring a new dimension into your awareness — to show what an ideal king is like — He staged such a great drama. In the same way was the life of Shri Krishna, and in the same way is the life of Jesus Christ. But throughout the whole of their lives, if you look closely, only one thing stands out — and that is your foolishness, your supreme folly.
Because of which you sent so great a principle to the cross. That is, the foolishness was exposed to such an extent, so tremendous was the folly that came to light, that at that time it was even said that if, in His place — if there is a thief, let the thief be crucified instead — that either the thief should be released or He. So these Jews, who today consider themselves to be so very clever, they said, "No, give Jesus Christ to the cross." Today you know what the condition of these Jews is, in what wretchedness they have been trapped. So great a sin have they committed from time immemorial — can it ever be washed away?
Even now people remain caught in that very ego, caught in that very same ego, and even now they still think that the sinful deeds and the things they have done were some great act of merit. Even now, if they were to ask forgiveness from God — saying that we crucified Your very principle, we destroyed that great principle which was Yours, O Lord, forgive us — even now they could be forgiven. But for a human being, to ask for forgiveness is a very difficult thing. He goes on committing one wickedness after another.
Who is there in this world who can say that they welcomed the saints? Look at Nanak Sahib — how terribly he was tormented; look at Kabir — how he was persecuted. No great person of this world has ever departed without suffering at the hands of this world, and those who inflict that suffering are human beings.
But now the world has changed, I am telling you. The Satya Yuga has begun. You may try and see for yourselves — now no one can inflict suffering upon any saint or sage. Enough has happened; now that is not going to go on any longer. The reason is that Jesus Christ has set in motion a very great power into the world, so that now any wicked person, any tormentor, may be taught a lesson. His Ekadasha Rudras have today become fully armed and ready. And whoever tries to torment any saint or sage will be utterly destroyed. To try to torment any saint or sage — this is a great sin.
This you must understand, and from the example of Jesus Christ you must learn, so that we do not commit this folly again. If we commit this folly, then we will have failed, and we will be finished forever.
Now, if any man calls himself an Incarnation, he should not be accepted as an Incarnation — this is certain. If every single man says "I am an Incarnation, I am an Incarnation," he must not be believed. In our own scriptures the criteria are laid down; looking at those criteria, if that Incarnation is proved genuine, then he should be accepted.
Someone brought me the news that Jesus Christ had been born again. I said, "Oh really, where did this happen?" They said, "He is in Rome." I said, "I see, and what does he do?" They said, "He says, 'I am Jesus Christ.'" So I said, "In that way even a donkey can say so. It is hardly the case that just because someone says 'I am Jesus Christ' he will become Jesus Christ." They said, "All right then, what is the mark by which one recognizes Jesus Christ?" I said, "Tell him to walk on water. Tell him now to stop the wind, the water, the storm. If he can stop them, and if he can walk on water, and if he can bring the dead back to life, then he is Jesus Christ. If he cannot do this, then he is not Jesus Christ." I said, "Keep giving just that; that very thing is good for you." Then someone said, "I had gone to cure someone's eyes." Let them keep doing it — there is no harm in it. You do it — do it as an official, or do it as a human being, do it as a social worker. But this is never the work of God. You attach every single thing to God.
I have even seen this — Sainath Bidi! There should be some limit to everything. You used to attach every single thing to God — Sadhana Soap! Just imagine, is there no end to it! And Vishnu-brand asafoetida! Lakshmi-brand asafoetida! Just imagine! Have these people made a mockery of God? And what benefit is going to come to you from this? They say, "Mother, we do it because it will be auspicious for us." Did it become auspicious? — "We will earn more money." You are never going to earn it! If you do your business in this way, you will face great suffering. This is an insult to Shri Lakshmi Herself — that they turned Her into asafoetida!
In the very same way, a human being does many such things, all of which amount to misappropriation. And in exactly this way, we have played this very same game with Jesus Christ as well. We should understand that Jesus Christ is neither anyone's property nor anyone's possession. He came into this world for the redemption and welfare of all of humankind; He is the Omkara principle of God. And it was this Omkara principle that offered itself as a sacrifice — only He can offer such a sacrifice, no one else.
All the other Incarnations who came afterward — their bodies were made of the earth element, but His body was made of the Pranava principle, and that is why His resurrection took place. Now, after the resurrection had happened, all those who were His disciples accepted, "Yes, indeed, He truly was what He said He was," because they had seen Him in His bodily form — only then did they believe. But then who was to come to give them their realization?
Then they set about doing all sorts of odd things — doing this, doing that, all kinds of things from all over the world — and people began to shout and scream in the name of Jesus. When they started all this screaming and shouting, evil spirits entered into them. And they began to speak in all kinds of languages and to say all kinds of things, and they have written this down in the Bible.
Now everyone thinks that this very entering of a spirit within oneself is itself the blessing of the Divine Spirit.
